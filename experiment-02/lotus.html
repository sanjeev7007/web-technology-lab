Lotus (Nelumbo nucifera)

The Sacred Water Flower

Introduction

Lotus is an aquatic plant known for its large, beautiful, and fragrant flowers. It is culturally and religiously significant in many Asian countries. The plant belongs to the family Nelumbonaceae and is native to Asia, especially India and China.

Botanical Description

  • Scientific Name: Nelumbo nucifera
  • Family: Nelumbonaceae
  • Type: Aquatic perennial plant
  • Leaves: Circular, large, floating above water
  • Flowers: Large, fragrant, pink or white petals
  • Fruits: Nut-like seeds called lotus seeds

Uses

  • Ornamental: Water gardens and ponds
  • Religious & Cultural: Sacred symbol in Hinduism and Buddhism
  • Medicinal: Seeds and roots used in traditional medicine
  • Culinary: Lotus seeds, roots, and stems are edible
  • Symbolism: Purity, enlightenment, and rebirth

Interesting Facts

  • Lotus seeds can remain viable for hundreds of years.
  • The lotus flower can regulate its temperature, like a warm-blooded animal.
  • It is the national flower of India and Vietnam.
Lotus: A symbol of purity, enlightenment, and beauty across cultures.
